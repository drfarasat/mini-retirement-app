A few quick questions.

Is this actually free?

Yes — completely free. The calculator runs in Google Sheets (no software needed) and the cheatsheet is a PDF. Both are yours the moment you sign up.

What exactly is a mini-retirement?

A mini-retirement is a deliberate pause — not quitting forever, but a funded hiatus to reinvent. You step off the treadmill, strip away what's non-essential, and use the time to explore what comes next. It's particularly suited to people in their 40s who have savings but not yet a full retirement portfolio.

I already have savings and investments. Is this still useful?

Especially if you do. The calculator is designed for people who already have something saved — £100K to £600K+ — and want to understand how long that runway actually is. Most people dramatically underestimate what their existing portfolio could support.

Is this relevant if I'm based outside the UK?

Yes — the concepts behind your FI number (the 4% rule, savings rate, compound growth) apply globally. The calculator uses straightforward maths that works wherever you are.
